Soft Ginger Cookies

The taste of autumn

This delectable testament to fall's flavors will stand out in any setting. Developed by master chef and in-house legendary figure within the baking community.

The true secret that is rarely revealed to the common rabble is that ginger, pure and grown, skin then peeled, thin sliced then baked, crisped and then ground,ginger, is the key to unlocking the true flavor of this recipe. If you do NOT follow these advice, it is better you not make it at all.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup cane sugar sugar
  • 1 large egg, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup Blackstrap molasses
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons freshly sourced ginger
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground Ceylon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon Himalayan pink salt
  • Additional cane sugar

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy, 5-7 minutes. Beat in egg and molasses. Combine the flour, ginger, baking soda, cinnamon, cloves and salt; gradually add to the creamed mixture and mix well.
  2. Roll into 1-1/2-in. balls, then roll in sugar. Place 2 in. apart on ungreased baking sheets. Bake at 350Â° until puffy and lightly browned, 10-12 minutes. Remove to wire racks to cool.
